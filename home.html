State of Virginia Constitution:
Article VIII. Education

Information on each article from constitution


    Section 1. Public schools of high quality to be maintained

  • Free public education at the elementary and secondary level is appointed by the General Assembly ensuring that each student throughout the commonwealth is guaranteed an upkeep educational program.

    Section 2. Standards of quality; State and local support of public schools

  • The Board of Education will establish and impose the quality standards for each school division throughout the commonwealth; the General Assembly has the authority to alter or revise these standards. The General Assembly decides how to pay for the upkeep of a school curriculum that satisfies the standards set and they will also decide how program expenses between the Commonwealth and local government that make up the school division. Each local government is responsible for covering its share of these costs through local taxes and other available money.

    Section 3. Compulsory education; free textbooks

  • Every eligible student of the right age, as defined by law, shall get an essential education in elementary and secondary school provided by the General Assembly. Textbooks are provided at no cost to every public school student.

    Section 4. Board of Education

  • There will be a nine-member Board of Education that will be appointed by the Governor and approved by the General Assembly. Each member appointed will serve for four years, except for the ones who fill vacancies shall be there for an unexpired term. Terms shall be staggered so that no more than three regular appointments can be arranged in the same year.

    Section 5. Powers and duties of the Board of Education

    The powers and duties of the Board of Education shall be as follows:

  • A. The Board of Education shall divide the Commonwealth into school divisions of such geographic area and school-age populations as will promote the realization of the prescribed standards of quality, subject to the criteria and conditions from the General Assembly. They will also review whether the current school divisions are sufficient for this purpose.

  • B. The Board will identify in yearly reports to the Governor and General Assembly the conditions and needs of public education in the Commonwealth. They will also identify any school that failed to establish and maintain the prescribed standards of quality.

  • C. A list of qualified applicants for the role of division superintendent of schools will be presented to the school board of each division and they will choose one candidate to fill that position. If the division school board is unable to select a division superintendent within the prescribed time by law, the Board of Education shall appoint someone.

  • D. The Board will that the power to approve textbooks instructional aids and materials for use in courses in the Commonwealth public schools.

  • E. The Board shall have the primary responsibility and authority to implement the educational policy outlined in this article, subject to the ultimate authority of the General Assembly. They also shall have other powers and duties as may be established by law.

    Section 6. Superintendent of Public Instruction

  • The Governor may appoint an experienced educator to be the Superintendent of Public Instruction, subject to confirmation by the General Assembly, for a term that aligns with the Governor’s declaration of the appointment. The General Assembly may alter the selection process and longevity of the position by statute. The powers and duties of the Superintendent shall be prescribed by law.

    Section 7. School boards

  • Each school division will be governed by a school board, which will be made up of members selected in the manner, for the term, possessing the qualifications, and to the number provided by law.

    Section 8. The Literary Fund

  • The General Assembly will set aside money from different sources like donations, fines, and interest earned on the Literacy Fund to create this fund. They can also add more money to the fund if it already has at least eighty million dollars, this money will be used for public schools including teachers’ retirement fund.
  • The proceeds from the sale of all property seized and forfeited to the Commonwealth for violation of criminal laws within the Commonwealth prohibiting the manufacture, sale, or distribution of marijuana or any controlled substance may be exempt from this section by general law provided by the General Assembly. The state treasury will receive these earnings, which will then be distributed by legislation to support law enforcement.
  • The General Assembly may authorize the Board to borrow other funds against assets of the Literary Fund as collateral. Important to note that this borrowing will not involve the full guarantee of the Commonwealth credit.
  • The principal of the Fund shall include assets of the Fund in other funds or authorities which are repayable to the Fund.

    Section 9. Other educational institutions

  • Any educational institution that is desired for the intellectual, cultural, and professional development of the people in the Commonwealth may be established, maintained, and operated by the General Assembly. Institutions should be governed in accordance with the law, as shall the statutes and powers of their boards of visitors or other governing bodies.

    Section 10. State appropriations prohibited to schools or institutions of learning not owned or exclusively controlled by the State or some subdivision thereof; exceptions to rule

  • Public funds cannot be given to schools or learning institutions that are not owned or controlled by the government with a few exceptions. The General assembly and local bodies can allocate funds for educational purposes to public and non-religious private schools, in addition to government-owned schools. The General Assembly can also provide funds to agencies or schools that are jointly established by multiple states for education purposes. Counties, cities, towns, and districts can allocate funds to non-religious schools that focus on manual, industrial, or technical training, as well as to schools that they own or control.

    Section 11. Aid to nonpublic higher education

  • The General Assembly has the authority to offer financial assistance to students attending nonprofit colleges and universities in the Commonwealth. These institutions should primarily focus on providing collegiate or graduate education rather than religious or theological training. The General Assembly can also establish a State agency or authority to help these institutions borrow money for constructing educational facilities. The Commonwealth will not be responsible for any debts incurred through such borrowing. The General Assembly can arrange contracts between the Commonwealth or its subdivisions and these institutions for educational or related services.
